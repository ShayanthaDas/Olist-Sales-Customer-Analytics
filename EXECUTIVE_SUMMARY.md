Olist Sales & Customer Analytics

Executive Summary



1. Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end business intelligence analysis of an e-commerce marketplace using the Olist dataset. The objective was to transform raw transactional data into structured insights that support strategic decision-making across revenue, customer behavior, operations, and regional performance.

The analysis follows a complete data lifecycle including data cleaning, integration, exploratory analysis, KPI development, and dashboard reporting. The final outcome is a set of business insights and recommendations designed to improve profitability, customer satisfaction, and operational efficiency.



2. Business Context

E-commerce platforms operate in a highly competitive and data-intensive environment where success depends on understanding customers, optimizing logistics, and managing product performance effectively.

In such systems, leadership teams typically face challenges such as:

* Identifying key revenue drivers
* Understanding customer purchase behavior
* Managing delivery performance and fulfillment delays
* Evaluating product category performance
* Improving customer retention and satisfaction

Without structured analytics, these decisions often rely on fragmented reporting or intuition, which limits growth potential and operational efficiency.

This project addresses these challenges using a data-driven approach.



3. Analytical Approach

The project was structured into five major phases:

3.1 Data Integration

Multiple datasets covering orders, customers, products, payments, sellers, and reviews were integrated into a unified analytical model.

3.2 Data Cleaning and Preparation

Missing values, inconsistencies, and formatting issues were handled to ensure data reliability.

3.3 Feature Engineering

Key business metrics such as delivery delay, revenue per order, and customer purchase frequency were created.

3.4 Exploratory Analysis

Trends and patterns were identified across revenue, customer segments, geography, and product categories.

3.5 Dashboard Development

Interactive dashboards were designed to visualize KPIs and support executive-level decision-making.



4. Key Business Insights

4.1 Revenue Concentration

Revenue is heavily concentrated in a limited number of product categories, indicating dependency on a small set of drivers.

4.2 Customer Contribution

Repeat customers contribute a significant portion of total revenue, highlighting the importance of retention strategies.

4.3 Geographic Performance Variation

Certain regions significantly outperform others in terms of sales volume and order frequency.

4.4 Operational Performance Impact

Delivery delays negatively influence customer satisfaction scores, indicating a direct relationship between logistics performance and customer experience.

4.5 Product Performance Differences

Some product categories consistently outperform others in both revenue generation and customer ratings.



5. Strategic Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

5.1 Strengthen Customer Retention

Develop loyalty programs and personalized marketing strategies to increase repeat purchases and customer lifetime value.

5.2 Optimize Product Portfolio

Focus on scaling high-performing product categories while reassessing underperforming segments.

5.3 Improve Delivery Operations

Work with logistics partners to reduce delivery delays and improve fulfillment efficiency.

5.4 Expand High-Growth Regions

Invest in targeted marketing and supply chain improvements in regions showing strong demand potential.

5.5 Implement KPI Monitoring System

Develop centralized dashboards for continuous tracking of revenue, customer behavior, and operational performance.



6. Expected Business Impact

If implemented effectively, these recommendations can lead to:

* Increased revenue through better product and customer targeting
* Improved customer retention and lifetime value
* Higher customer satisfaction scores
* Reduced operational inefficiencies
* Better regional market penetration
* More informed and faster decision-making



7. Conclusion

This project demonstrates how raw transactional data can be transformed into actionable business intelligence through structured analysis and visualization.

It reflects the practical responsibilities of a Business Analyst or Data Analyst, including KPI development, customer behavior analysis, and strategic recommendation generation.

The insights generated are directly applicable to real-world e-commerce decision-making and demonstrate the value of data-driven strategy in improving business performance.
