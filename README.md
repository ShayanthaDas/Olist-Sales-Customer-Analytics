Olist Sales & Customer Analytics: End-to-End Business Intelligence Project

Project Overview

Organizations today generate vast amounts of transactional and customer data. However, data alone does not create value unless it can be transformed into actionable insights that support business decisions. This project was developed to demonstrate how sales and customer data can be analyzed to uncover revenue drivers, customer purchasing behavior, operational bottlenecks, and growth opportunities.

Using the Olist e-commerce dataset, this project follows a complete analytics lifecycle, beginning with raw data preparation and ending with business recommendations supported by interactive dashboards and analytical findings.

The primary objective was not simply to create charts or reports, but to replicate the work of a Business Analyst or Data Analyst in a real-world business environment. Throughout the project, emphasis was placed on understanding business performance, identifying trends, evaluating customer behavior, and generating recommendations that could realistically be presented to management.



Business Problem

E-commerce businesses operate in highly competitive environments where customer retention, operational efficiency, and revenue growth are critical to long-term success.

Management often faces challenges such as:

* Understanding which products generate the highest revenue
* Identifying profitable customer segments
* Monitoring delivery performance
* Measuring customer satisfaction
* Detecting geographic sales trends
* Improving customer retention

Without proper analysis, decision-makers may struggle to prioritize investments, allocate resources efficiently, or identify emerging opportunities.

This project addresses these challenges by transforming raw transactional data into meaningful business intelligence.

Project Workflow

Phase 1: Data Collection

The project began by importing multiple relational datasets containing customer, order, product, payment, and review information.

Phase 2: Data Cleaning

Data quality checks were performed to identify:

* Missing values
* Duplicate records
* Inconsistent formatting
* Invalid dates
* Data type issues

The datasets were then cleaned and standardized for analysis.

Phase 3: Data Integration

Multiple tables were joined to create a unified analytical dataset capable of supporting cross-functional business analysis.

Phase 4: Exploratory Data Analysis

Exploratory analysis was conducted to identify:

* Revenue trends
* Customer purchasing patterns
* Product popularity
* Regional performance differences
* Customer satisfaction indicators

Phase 5: KPI Development

Business metrics were created including:

* Total Revenue
* Average Order Value
* Customer Lifetime Value
* Monthly Sales Growth
* Delivery Performance
* Customer Satisfaction Rate

Phase 6: Dashboard Development

Interactive dashboards were developed to enable stakeholders to monitor performance and make data-driven decisions.

Phase 7: Business Recommendation Generation

Analytical findings were translated into strategic recommendations focused on revenue growth, customer retention, and operational improvement.



Key Insights

Several important insights emerged during the analysis.

Revenue Concentration

A relatively small number of product categories generated a significant proportion of total sales revenue.

Customer Segmentation Opportunity

Customer purchasing behavior revealed opportunities for segmentation and targeted marketing initiatives.

Geographic Expansion Potential

Certain regions demonstrated stronger purchasing activity and may represent attractive growth opportunities.

Delivery Performance Impact

Order fulfillment delays appeared to influence customer satisfaction ratings.

Retention Opportunities

Repeat customers contributed disproportionately to business performance, highlighting the importance of retention-focused strategies.
